R-FORCE - Experience true zero gravity

R-Force is the ONLY technology that enables gait training with full body-weight support. Air pressure can reduce effective body weight by up to 100%, allowing training already in the early phase of recovery.

How it works: pressure chamber

The enclosure acts as a pressure chamber. Pressure is distributed evenly around the body and supports the patient naturally during gait training.

Full unloading up to 100%

With body-weight support, patients can be mobilised early and move naturally despite reduced load.

Early start of training

Gait training can begin already in the first recovery phase - safe, progressive and individually dosed.

Motivating games & progress tracking

Visual biofeedback

The integrated wide-angle camera provides a real-time gait sequence. Patients can see and correct their gait directly.

Motivating training games

Game-based exercises help improve balance and walking ability while maintaining motivation throughout therapy.

Save & export data

Gait parameters can be stored and exported to objectively track and evaluate patient progress.

Neurological & orthopaedic use

Ideal for chronic stroke and orthopaedic indications that need progressive, unloaded gait rehabilitation.

Clinical evidence from published studies

  • Metabolic rate: Studies report increased metabolic parameters during anti-gravity treadmill training in neurological patients.
  • Therapy course: Comparisons in post-operative and post-infectious conditions show measurable changes between first and last sessions.
  • Gait asymmetry: Body-weight-supported treadmill programs show positive effects on gait parameters after lower-limb arthroplasty.
  • Objective documentation: Assessments are based on objectively captured parameters and support transparent progress evaluation.

R-Force Therapy FAQ

Who is the ideal candidate for R-Force?

R-Force is ideal for outpatient chronic stroke and orthopaedic patients who need unloaded, step-by-step gait training.

When should R-Force therapy begin after an injury?

Because R-Force enables full body-weight support, gait training can start already in the early phase of recovery.

How should R-Force therapy be dosed and how many sessions are needed?

Therapy can range from daily sessions in acute cases to once-weekly sessions in chronic cases.

How is therapy intensity adjusted?

Intensity should be adapted to physical capacity and progressive increases in step count and walking distance, always considering patient condition.
